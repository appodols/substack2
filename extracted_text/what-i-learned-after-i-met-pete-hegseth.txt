In mid-2021, I went on Jordan Petersons podcast. All the reading and thinking and writing Id done had inadvertently prepared me for that podcast. As of today it has 1.4 million views and people still write to me about it.
Then I signed a deal with Substack in April of 2022. The original version of this newsletter was on MailChimp, and I subsequently moved it over here to Substack.
Many people have misconceptions about fame.
After I went on Fox that first time, people thought it was a huge deal. My friends from high school were blown away. Some friends at Yale thought my life was going to change. But really, it was a flash-in-the-pan situation. Signing with a literary agency was a big deal, but they couldnt even find a publisher who would take a meeting to buy my book. I was a dude who nobody had ever heard of, and I had no celebrities or established authors championing me. Writing an op-ed in the biggest newspaper and appearing on the most-watched cable news channel might have been enough to change your life twenty or thirty years ago. But in todays era of never-ending content, fragmented audiences, short attention spans, and limitless competition for attention, the more likely outcome is being known for a week or so and then dropping off peoples radar.
Nowadays, essays and podcasts and social media followings have staying power. People will listen to and reference a podcast from four years ago. Naval Ravikants appearance on Rogan (and the episode with Matthew Walker on sleep) is still cited in conversations I have both on and offline. This is why no one recognizes me from Fox but they do from the podcasts Ive been on. I only signed a book deal after Id amassed a relatively large online presence.
