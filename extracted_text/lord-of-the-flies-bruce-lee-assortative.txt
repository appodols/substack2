Ive never seen anything like this either. Research from Gregory Clarksuggestsassortative mating (the trend whereby people choose spouses who are similar to themselves not just in terms of appearance, but in terms of education, intellect, and class background) has endured for centuries and hasnt changed all that much. People point to the mid-twentieth century and speak about male executives marrying their secretaries. But Clarks work suggests that smarter executives tended to marry smarter secretaries. In terms of occupational prestige, there was a gap. But underlying abilities still drew people together. Whats on your resume doesnt seem to matter as much as whats in your head; though nowadays, as social barriers have dissolved, those two things are more tightly correlated than before. People are pretty good at picking up on these invisible attributes when seeking a long-term partner. This is why I think males dropping out of higher ed wont have much of an effect on assortative mating. Women can still detect a mans intellect and overall competence by relying on signals other than educational credentials.

For what its worth, Ive noticed my own long-term relationship history reflects this assortative mating pattern along class lines:
When I was in the military at age 21, I dated a girl who was studying to be a nurse. A couple years later when I was reassigned to Europe, I became a bit more curious and open to the possibility of attending college. There, I entered a relationship with a student at a prominent German university. She boosted my confidence in my own academic ability when at one point she offhandedly remarked, I dont think any of my uni friends are as well read as you (to be fair, that probably says more about the educational system than about me). At Yale, as an undergrad, I dated a grad student. Then, in grad school, I met my current girlfriend who was also studying at Cambridge. There is no doubt in my mind she is more intelligent than me. As a final note (heres a bonus interesting finding), there is typicallyabouta 10 point IQ gap between spouses.

Maybe you've moved to the big city and don't have your small town network anymore. If you want to *preserve*, let alone boost your status, it's much more important to find a high-status husband than it would be today. Well where are you going to find him?
