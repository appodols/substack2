[In square brackets are thoughts/notes I jotted while listening]:
Jordan suggests agreeableness is the most conceptually complicated personality dimension because there are pronounced advantages and disadvantages for every point on the agreeableness distribution.
Agreeable people are good-natured (vs. irritable), soft hearted (vs. ruthless), courteous (vs. rude), forgiving (vs. vengeful), sympathetic (vs. callous).
Agreeableness encompasses two lower order aspects: Compassion and politeness.
Both of these sound like good traits, but its important to understand that there is no universally good (or adaptive) personality trait.
[Evolution has not selected for a single optimal personality profile because no such profile exists across all possible varied landscapes. The material and social environment is constantly shifting. Personality Profile A might be optimal in Environment X whereas Personality Profile B might be better suited for Environment Y.]
Characteristics of a person high on compassion: interested in other peoples problems, feels others emotions, inquires about others well-being, bothered about others needs, sympathizes with others feelings, takes time for others, takes an interest in other peoples lives, has a soft side, likes to do things for others.
You can think of compassion as a negotiating stance. The more compassionate you are, the more likely you are to negotiate on the other persons behalf rather than your own. [If you are extremely compassionate, you might be more exploitable].
This is relevant to understanding gender differences in this trait.
[Women are, on average, more agreeable and compassionate because in the ancestral environment, women evolved to care for the neediest and most vulnerable creature of allthe human infant. Babies without compassionate and agreeable mothers did not receive care and were far more likely to die. I once heard Mikhaila Peterson describe infants as a wet noodle with a heavy head. Babies are so fragile. It is a miracle humans manage to shepherd any of them through in non-industrialized hunter-gatherer societies.]
Jordan suggests you can think of politeness as akin to respect for authority. Conservatives tend to be higher on politeness and lower on compassion; liberals tend to be higher on compassion and lower on politeness. Broadly speaking, liberals are compassionate and conservatives are polite.

Characteristics of polite people: respect for authority, doesnt believe they are better than others, hates to seem pushy, doesnt take advantage of others, avoids imposing their will on others, rarely puts people under pressure, doesnt insult others, doesnt seek conflict, not out for their own personal gain.
Jordan points out that people often conflate extraversion with positive regard. A person might be fun to be around and sociable, but that doesnt necessarily mean they like you as an individual. Rather, they enjoy attention and the social energy from being around others. Of course, they might like you as well, but just because a person is generally extraverted does not mean they feel especially warm toward you.
One of the biggest sex differences in personality is in agreeableness. Women are higher. Another big sex difference is in neuroticism. Women are also higher.
Maternal impulses are likely responsible for this sex difference. In the ancestral environment, it was adaptive for mothers to be relatively high in agreeableness and its associated qualities of nurture and care, and also relatively high in neuroticism and its associated qualities of sensitivity to potential external threats.
Jordan speculates that womens emotional system is optimized not solely for the individual, but rather for the mother-infant dyad. [The idea is that in the ancestral environment, evolutionary pressures shaped women to be hyper-responsive to potential threats on behalf of not only themselves, but for their dependent and vulnerable infants as well.]
The extreme agreeable end is maternal behavior, and on the extreme disagreeable end is predatory behavior.
Humans have evolved for care and nurturing (associated with maternal behavior) but we also evolved for hunting and warfare (associated with predatory behavior).
Many men who are capable of predatory aggression are also capable of supplying maternal care. Both capacities can exist in the same individual and each can be expressed or suppressed depending on the social context.
There can be downsides to extremely high agreeableness. One is dependent personality disorder. One feature of this is the inability to establish autonomy to act independently. A childlike pattern of dependence on authority figures that extends well into adulthood. An individual with this condition lacks the inner drive to go out into the world and establish and sustain themselves.
Agreeableness becomes counterproductive when it interferes with the development of your own autonomy.
It can also be counterproductive when you treat individuals who are capable of autonomy as targets of your own maternal behavior. [For example, an overly nurturing parent who continues to baby their child long past when it is age-appropriate to do so. The devouring mother archetype.]
[Compassion is vulnerable to exploitation by psychopaths. In a society that is agreeable, empathic, and egalitarian, a malevolent predator has free reign to take advantage of others kindness.]
Two things clinical psychologists spend most of their time doing: The first is treating depression, anxiety, and negative emotions. The second is assertiveness training.
Clinical psychologists help their clients work to advance their own interests in a more effective way. They help their clients to become a bit more extraverted and a bit more disagreeable to negotiate on their own behalf more effectively.
[Relatedly, agreeable people tend to have a relativelylowdesire for status. They are less likely to self-promote and boast about their accomplishments in social situations.]

Excessive agreeableness and dependent personality disorder often mask intense underlying hostility and resentment. This expresses itself in passive aggressive behavior.
This dependent personality profile might be increasing on university campuses, as affluent young people have become less autonomous and more dependent for a longer period on their parents.
[Mary Harrington has pointed out that many children in upper and upper middle class families are raised by nannies or expensive day care or other adult figures who are not their parents. Such caregivers tend to be extra cautious about how much risk they allow the children under their supervision to take because there are liable if the child is injured. For example, while a mother might let her child play on the monkey bars even if it is a little bit dangerous, a nanny will only allow the child to engage in safe activities. As a result, the kid never learns to test himself and never discovers how resilient he is. This may be one reason why the current generation of young people, raised to be cocooned in safety, now believe that anything that deviates from this represents existential danger.]
You have to take risks to become autonomous.
Jordan also suggests that smaller families might play a role too. Children in past generations had lots of siblings. This has been the historical norm. Nowadays many parents have only one child or two, which might contribute to the preoccupation with safety. Elsewhere Jordan hassuggestedthat siblings tend to knock the narcissism out of each other.

Agreeable children do better in school than disagreeable children, even controlling for intelligence. Agreeable kids are less prone to delinquency and criminality.
Low agreeableness is a predictor of incarceration, as is low conscientiousness.
Competitive sports are abstracted hunting competitions. Youre in a hunting party, and you identify a target and try to hit it. Soccer, basketball, football, etc. It involves chasing something (the ball; opposing team players), and throwing projectiles (the ball).

Disagreeableness, if socialized properly, contributes to the competitive spirit that helps to attain goals.
