Rob, It takes courage to do what you are doing. I try to focus on how to ask the right questions, the ones that help get you to a meaningful place, and to the next question. With many "ah ha" moments along the way. I just finished reading "Troubled". I'm excited to be able to watch and see what's next.  I do believe living in meaningful connection with others is what makes life meaningful, at least for me. Like the tapestry analogy, those around you are like threads in the tapestry, various threads will become frayed here and there, so what are the little things (they are usually little, being there for someone, being present to another person rather than thinking what am I going to say, etc) that can help mend those threads or at least plant a seed of hope and make that tapestry, one's social world, stronger and more meaningful. Others have said hope and daring to dream about the future and the hope that is possible for that future (a creative state), the opposite of the destructive and hate phases of our society, and learning from the past (through the entire arc of history), is what brings wisdom. I am glad to see the old ancient "virtues" getting some attention.  Like Jeffrey Rosen's new book "The pursuit of happiness: How classical writers on virtue inspired the lives of the founders and defined America"... From the back cover: Virtue requires regular cultivation... A life invested in understanding the past may in fact be a happier one... How "the pursuit of happiness" never meant pleasure or success, but the self-governing quest, always unachieved, of virtue.
