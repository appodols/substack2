Conscientiousness can be understood as being careful (versus careless), reliable (vs. undependable), well-organized (vs. disorganized), conscientious (vs. negligent), well-disciplined (vs. weak-willed), persevering (vs. quitting)
As with the other personality traits, Conscientiousness can be broken down into 2 aspects: Industriousness and orderliness
People who are high on industriousness: carry out their plans, finish what they start, dont waste their time, dont find it difficult to start work, dont tend to mess things up, put their mind at the task at hand, get things done quickly, know what they are doing, dont postpone decisions, are not easily distracted.
To be clear,carryingout plans is a hallmark of industriousness, not simply making plans.
To be clear,
out plans is a hallmark of industriousness, not simply making plans.
People who are high on orderliness: dont leave their belongings around, like order, keep things tidy, follow a schedule, bothered by messy people, want everything to be just right, bothered by disorder, like routine, see that rules are observed, want every detail taken care of.
Industrious people value duty, diligence, and focus
Orderly people value organizational ability, concentration on detail, and insistence on boundaries and borders
Conscientiousness exhibits the same issue asagreeableness: its straightforwardly obvious why it would be advantageous to be high on this trait, but not so obvious why being low on this trait might be helpful.

After intelligence, conscientiousness is the second best predictor of life outcomes
This is especially true in managerial and administrative positions
Given these facts, it is difficult to understand why there would be so much variation on this trait such that people would score low on it
Jordan suggests it helps to understand the pathologies associated with being overly high on Conscientiousness
[Being very high on this trait might inhibit risk taking. Risk taking, at least in some contexts, was beneficial in the ancestral environment.]
Self-reports for Conscientiousness are generally relatively valid. You can also get an accurate picture of someones Conscientiousness from peer reports (asking the persons friends, family, spouse, coworkers, etc. about the persons level of Conscientiousness). This is true for each Big Five personality trait.
When you combine self-report with peer reports, you can get an even better sense of a persons personality compared with just self-report or peer report alone.
This is because you know things about yourself that others dont know, and others know things about you that you dont know
Your prefrontal cortex is responsible for planning and thinking. Your motor cortex is responsible for voluntary activity and movement.
Over the course of evolutionary history, the motor cortex came first, and then the prefrontal cortex grew out of the motor cortex
This means that the region of your brain that thinks and plans is essentially an extension of your brain region that acts and moves. Your prefrontal cortex plans and considers alternative scenarios based on various potential actions and movements you make. If I act this way, what is the likely outcome? What if I move that way, what is likely to happen?
Jordan suggests that this might be why stories are so appealing. We get to learn about characters who make certain decisions, and observe their outcomes.
[Ive written elsewhere that Fiction allows us to take different selves out for a test drive, allow them some expression, but we can safely return to our structured, ordinary lives.]
The correlation between intelligence and conscientiousness is zero.
[Jordan express surprise at this. Anecdotally, I have known several very smart but lazy people, and many hardworking but academically unexceptional people. In high school I knew a guy who played for the football team, an athlete and a hard worker, who spent 6 months studying to take the ASVAB (military standardized test) and failed it twice. In the Air Force I knew a guy who was very smart, smarter than me, who simply had no interest in exerting any more effort than absolutely necessary to please our superiors, and dodged any attempts at promotion because he didnt want any additional responsibilities.]
Jordan describes the attempts he and his colleagues made to produce experiments in the psychology lab that would accurately assess conscientiousness and each attempt was met with failure.
The correlation between delayed gratification in a lab setting and Conscientiousness is zero.
No one has been able to create a physical task that accurately measures Conscientiousness. All measures of this trait still rely on self or other report.
[To be clear, self-reported measures of Conscientiousness correlate with income, education, etc. But self-reported measures of Conscientiousness dont correlate at all with lab experiments.]

Political conservatives are higher than liberals on orderliness. Orderliness is also associated with sensitivity to disgust.
Parasite prevalence predicts a preference for political authoritarianism.
[This is because when there is a serious risk of contracting a deadly infectious disease, people are more likely to favor strict and rigid laws and norms of behavior to avert the possibility of dying.]
[This is an overlooked pattern in the modern developed world, where we have medical technology, vaccines, cleanliness, hygiene based on the germ theory of disease, and so on. Political liberalism is in many ways a byproduct of advancements in science and technology. Before the modern era, illness and infections were widespread. I wrote at length about thishere.]

Jordan cites research on predictors of authoritarianism. Warfare doesnt correlate with authoritarianism, contrary to what you might expect. Malnutrition has a very small correlation with authoritarianism. Famine moderately correlates with authoritarianism. We dont know the causal direction. It could be that hungry people favor authoritarian regimes, or it could be that authoritarian regimes are more likely to mismanage their economies and starve their people. Pathogen prevalence, however, has a striking correlation with authoritarianism (r = .46).

Orderliness correlates with disgust sensitivity. [This may be one reason why women score higher than men on orderliness; whereas men tend to score higher than women on industriousness. Orderliness is associated with pathogen avoidance, which is more important for women as the bearers of children.]
[When I was writing my PhD thesis I learned that when they are pregnant, womens immune systems become weaker as their bodies redirect resources to the growing infant. To compensate for their weakened immune systems, pregnant women subsequently become more sensitive to disgust and pathogens, in order to avoid anything that might lead to illness or infection.]
As syphilis became widespread throughout Europe, Victorian sexual morality arose, likely to guard against infection.
[Parents and societies have historically been more protective over daughters sexuality relative to sons. Pregnancy and paternal uncertainty are factors. But the threat of disease cant be overlooked. Relative to men, women face a greater risk of contracting an illness from sex. This is because the vagina has a larger surface area than the penis. As one example, when having heterosexual sex with a person who is HIV-positive, women are twice as likely as men to contract HIV.]
Norms and laws constraining sexual behavior seem to have arisen to prevent the spread of deadly illnesses.
People with anorexia are often hyper-conscientious. Its often referred to as a disease of upper-middle-class young women. Jordan suggests this is because genetically they are more likely to be Conscientiousness.
People with anorexia are often perfectionists and view any fat on their bodies as contaminants. It takes a lot of willpower to starve yourself. Jordan describes one of his clients who wanted to reduce her body down to the bone because she viewed this as pristine. People with anorexia are often judgmental toward others eating behaviors.
Jordan discusses old social psychology research suggesting that conservatives were more sensitive to threat than liberals. He is skeptical of this work.
[Much of this line of research hasnt replicated. I wonder if the conservative threat sensitivity finding was actually a sex difference. Up until the 1980s, women used to be more conservative than men. And now that women are more liberal, this might explain why the threat sensitivity finding has not replicated. In fact, some recent research has found that liberals are now more sensitive to threat than conservatives.]
On average, liberals are slightly more neurotic than conservatives.

Jordan suggests that extreme Conscientiousness, especially extreme orderliness, can degenerate into pathological political regimes.
German tanks in WWII were built to last for a long time, in contrast to American tanks which were rapidly cranked out in a more quick-and-dirty fashion. Regardless of how well they were built, tanks on both sides were typically destroyed quickly on the battlefield. Jordan suggests this was a pitfall of high Conscientiousness among the nazis, because they expended too many resources and too much attention on making their tanks exactly right. [The phrase done beats perfect comes to mind for this example.]
Jordan: You cant let your focus on the details interfere with your focus on the big picture, especially when the big picture changes.
People who have obsessive-compulsive personality disorder (OCPD) are preoccupied with cleanliness and order. This seems to be a manifestation of extreme Conscientiousness.
[OCPD involves not only a desire for cleanliness, butalsoa preoccupation with details, orderliness, and miserliness. Interestingly, OCPD positively correlates with socioeconomic status.]

[To be clear, obsessive-compulsive disorder is a separate condition from obsessive-compulsive personality disorder. Its confusing, I know. Basically, OCD is a debilitating mental health condition that involves intrusive and often unwanted thoughts involving repetitive behaviors. Handwashing, counting things, touching things, driving around in circles, etc. In contrast, OCPD is a personality disorder that involves excessive focus on order and perfection. Usually, in everyday life, when people refer to others or themselves as OCD, they mean OCPD.]
Jordan: Low Agreeable, high Conscientious types are interesting because you can trust them. Theyll do their duty but theyre blunt and harsh. That can be helpful because theyll tell you unpleasant truths. Conscientiousness takes the edge off low agreeableness.
[Theres a bit of a George Constanza element to being disagreeable and Conscientious, in that you learn to unthinkingly default to the opposite of your initial impulse.]
Longitudinal studies show that personality traits continue to develop throughout adulthood. There is a 1 standard deviation increase in Conscientiousness from adolescence to middle age.
[For low-skilled blue collar workers and manual laborers, Conscientiousness is a stronger predictor than IQ for earnings. This is likely because for rote physical jobs, intelligence doesnt help much but efficiency and diligence do help.]

One area where too much conscientiousness (the personality trait)  can lead to worse outcomes is in decision-making.  There is an optimal time for making a decision, and if you tend to procrastinate you will know all about what happens when you leave decision making too late.  Indeed, there is a strong tendency to say that procrastination is all about carelessness and laziness -- or about putting things off even though there are bad consequences for doing so.
One area where too much conscientiousness (the personality trait)  can lead to worse outcomes is in decision-making.  There is an optimal time for making a decision, and if you tend to procrastinate you will know all about what happens when you leave decision making too late.  Indeed, there is a strong tendency to say that procrastination is all about carelessness and laziness -- or about putting things off even though there are bad consequences for doing so.
What is less well known is that many bad business and life outcomes happen because people make decisions too quickly, and too soon.  There is a certain sort of tidy-minded person who absolutely cannot stand having things 'up in the air -- uncertain'.  Once 'we will need to make a decision about this' shows up on the horizon, they want to make the decision as soon as possible.  If there are dozens of things that need to be considered in making the decision, they want to think about them for as short an amount of time as possible.  Once a decision is made, they have a new plan, and they are happy.  And they will resist every effort to change the plan later.  They hate 'possibilities', and 'options' --- really, really hate having them and leaving them open.
