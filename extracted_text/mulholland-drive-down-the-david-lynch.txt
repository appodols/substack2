I joined Richard Hanania for a deep dive into the strange and surreal films of the late David Lynch, one of the most unique filmmakers of recent times. We talk about Mulholland Drive (2001) and Inland Empire (2006) and why one is mesmerizing while the other feels like cinematic torture. If you enjoy dissecting films that refuse to give easy answers, this conversation is for you. Many spoilers throughout.
Some topics we cover:
Mulholland Drive came out in 2001. Richard and I speak about how Hollywood's portrayal of lesbians in the early 2000s reflects a very different cultural moment compared to today.
Some tangents on Brokeback Mountain (2005) and other gay portrayals of that era
Why Mulholland Drive works despite its bizarre dream logic, and why Inland Empire is just a mess.
The cowboy test in Mulholland Drive. If you see him once, you did good. Twice, you did bad. What does it mean?
Richard shares his experience driving down Mulholland Drive and how the geography of Los Angeles shapes its mystique.
We break down the Harvey Weinstein subtext in Hollywood movies, including a wild meme about Quentin Tarantino thats very on the nose.
Was Mulholland Drive all just a dream? Did Diane really kill Camilla, or is there another way to interpret the movie? We debate different theories.
We get into Justin Therouxs character in Mulholland Drivewhat does his arc mean, and is he actually a part of Dianes psyche?
David Lynch loves mystery, but did he even have a concrete answer to what happens in Mulholland Drive? Or is it all just meant to be ambiguous?
That bizarre audition sceneis it a critique of Hollywoods predatory culture or something even darker?
Silencio! The club scene is unsettling, mesmerizing, and somehow central to the movies dream logic. We discuss what it might mean.
The symbolism of the dumpster monster behind the dinerone of the most terrifying moments in the film.
We debate whether Diane and Bettys identities blur because of subconscious guilt, or if theres another explanation.
Betty's world is more "real" than Diane'swhy does the dream feel coherent while the supposed reality is surreal and chaotic?
We then shift over to Inland Empire which was just about unwatchable, in my view. I share my very speculative opinion that the whole movie might be a womans fantasy of stardom and infidelity, and how it unravels.
The Lost Girls storyline mirrors Nikkis, suggesting a cycle of despair and entrapmentis she another version of Nikki, or a past self?
