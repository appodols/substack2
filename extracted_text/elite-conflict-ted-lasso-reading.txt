When my children were in high school, we read Hemingway's Farewell to Arms together. The first thing I asked them was to spot the metaphors in the very first scene of the book.  They struggled to do so. I pointed out that the fall of dust from the soldiers' boots onto the leaves of the nearby trees was like the fall of rain or snow on those same leaves, which suggests that warfare is a natural act (just like rain or snow) that is part of human nature and cannot be avoided. I remember my son asking me, "Did Hemingway understand what he was doing when he wrote that passage?"  My response was to smile and say, "Yes, he was a very smart man."
