I remember as a teenager working at a restaurant and later at a grocery store. Almost everyone I grew up with made 7 bucks an hour, 8 bucks an hour. There was a lot of discussion about how to get to 10 dollars an hour. Or how "if I could just get to 12 bucks an hour" what a huge difference that would make and how happy I'd be. At some point, when I was about 17, I sat in my rusted out 1992 Ford Escort in the parking lot before work, less than excited about clocking in. I looked at all the nicer vehicles nearby and thought "even if I get to 50 dollars an hour"which was unbelievableand managed to buy a better car, I still had to be at a specific location, work was still tied directly to clocking in and spending a precise amount of time there. It just seemed like a bad plan to be selling off parts of my life. I started to think about maybe, eventually, someday, getting some kind of job where it paid for my ideas, or creativity, or projects, or something other than how many specific hours I've logged at a set place. Then I sold off my entire life by enlisting in the Air Force for what then amounted to about $1300/month. That thought about time and money, though, lingered somewhere in my mind. My readers have given me the ability to escape the bonds of time to read and write and surface interesting ideas while developing and sharing my own. I am extremely thankful.
No Sunday post today, as Ive been traveling a lot. I was recently at a conference about academic freedom. I used to think it was ridiculous that these discussions even need to be had. Isnt the entire mission of a university or any research institution to pursue the truth, even if we dont like what we find? Isnt academic freedom necessary to do this? Gradually, though, Im realizing how counterintuitive the idea of free expression is to most people. And how vigilantly it must be guarded. There will, it seems, always be people and organized factions who will try to silence others for their own interests. Nothing comes without cost. Investing time, energy, and resources to safeguard the pursuit of truth is, sadly, a tax we must regularly pay to live in a free society.
