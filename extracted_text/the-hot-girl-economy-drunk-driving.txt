Good point here from Arnold Kling. Young people with college graduate parents typically have more status (relative to first generation students). But parents with kids in college also get a boost in status. When people talk about disrupting higher education or rethinking the college system, they should remember college isnt just about education. Eighteen year olds want to hang out with other young people, binge drink, hook up with strangers, etc. And will gladly go into debt in exchange for the status of being a college graduate.
Finally getting around to watching "The Last Dance" on Netflix, about the golden age of the Chicago Bulls. Interesting social class element in the series. At one point their #1 (Michael Jordan) and number #2 players (Scottie Pippen) were paid wildly different salaries. In fact, in his peak Pippen wasn't even in the top 100 highest paid players in the NBA. Jordan grew up solidly middle class. In contrast, Pippen grew up extremely poor, had family and several siblings he had to take care of, and negotiated his salary with outdated information and little leverage. So many people focus on gender/racial/etc. disparities in earnings, but this was a case where social class factors (poor negotiation, material anxiety, financial caution) mattered far more.
