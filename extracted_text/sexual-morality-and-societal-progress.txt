I just listened to the discussion. Once again, I am annoyed by the stereotype that poor people have more children because the welfare state incentivizes this.  Perhaps that is the case in the UK, but the welfare options in Cleveland, Ohio, where I have worked as a mental health counselor with low income clients for 30 years, are certainly not rewarding.  Any cash assistance is extremely low and limited to a few months, certainly not anything that you could support yourself and a child on.  You have to work unless you can depend on family to provide support.  You cannot depend on the father of the child to offer any support. You can get a voucher for child care so you can work, but,hopefully, you can keep your job long enough to wade through the red tape to get approved. You can get food stamps, but usually not  enough, especially if your low paying job pays you just a little too much.  Since you cannot afford rent with your low paying job, you can apply for subsidized housing if you can wait two to five years for the funds to be available. The one advantage is that you qualify for Medicaid which will pay for you to have a baby. But, again, if you make a little too much, you will lose that necessary support. But it doesnt cover an abortion, so maybe that is how the welfare state allows the poor to have more children. It has been rare for the poor women that I have worked with through the years to have more than one or two children.  So please stop referring to welfare as an explanation for why the poor have more children.  If they do, the reasons are not because welfare makes it so easy.
