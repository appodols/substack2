re: seller benefitting at the expense of the seller (and the reverse, when it comes to selling sex) -- I think this is based on an analysis of who has the power in the exchange. Economic models oftern assume that buyers and sellers have similar levels of power, each could walk away from the exchange, and each find the exchange beneficial.  But power-starved buyers are often desperate to obtain something, sometimes something which they cannot afford at all, but which their life circumstances says must be made, and right now as well.  People assume that these people are getting gouged, simply because the opportunity to do so is there.  (Also, when the items are later sold at a discount, many people assume that that lower price is the 'real cost', and not something substantially less than that because the seller is selling at a loss.) Prostitution is different, in that people's default assumption is that the seller is the desperate one, who doesn't have the power in the exchange.  This is not absolute -- there are people who insist that the wealthy successful top-sellers on Only-Fans are gouging their less powerful customers, rather then the reverse.
