What I found so clarifying is that reproductive strategy is coming from EVOLUTION, not from animals themselves, who are merely pawns of evolution. One of my main complaints about evo psych was that it seemed to grant agency to animals that they do not have. Now I see that it's evolution itself that has a kind of agency, in that its only goal is to produce more bodies. This makes sense to me, while I used to think evo psych claimed that animals sat around scheming about how best to produce more offspring. In fact, I don't think any animals other than humans really care about reproducing; they just do what nature compels them to do. We humans are also compelled to do many things, but we are clearly far more able to wrestle with evolutionary impulses than other animals. If we weren't, there would be no birth control. And there wouldn't be so many men who pressure their girlfriends into getting abortions (I saw a LOT of that when I volunteered at Planned Parenthood; evolution might make a lot of men promiscuous, but it doesn't make these same men want to become fathers).
