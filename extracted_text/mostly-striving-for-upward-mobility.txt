The commotion also reminds me of another quote:
There is a recognisable psychological type: bright enough to get into Harvard/Oxbridge, not super-bright, and able to play the games of diplomacy and chimp politics.

There is a charitable interpretation of the I went to college in Boston trope. Buried signals communicate information only to intended recipients. Only those "in the know" would understand Boston is coded language. It allows the speaker to identify other insiders while not attracting unwanted attention or being seen as a snob.
The Harvard president resigned in large part due to the tireless determination of Christopher Rufo. The defeated faction had to do something. Groping around, they settled on attacking Rufo for having a masters degree from the Harvard Extension School. Or they claimed that he was misrepresenting his credentials in some way (theres no evidence of this), and that he didnt go to the real Harvard. Or something. The underlying attack was that he was not one of them. Harvard Extension isnt as selective or prestigious as other graduate schools, and Rufos detractors decided this was a vulnerability for him.
This was a humorous error. It falls into what Robert Greene would call tactical but not strategic. Tacticians focus only the immediate obstacle before them, often with no overarching goal in mind. In contrast, strategists remember the bigger picture and the ultimate overarching goal that each decision is intended to accomplish.
Rufos critics wanted to diminish Rufo, without considering what their attacks would do to the perception of elite universities and to the reputation of their faction (cultural elites in legacy institutions).
The Harvard professor in the tweet above says Harvard Extension School students are without background. Shes not wrongits almost certainly true that people who attend the extension school come from families that are not as rich as the other graduate programs. But they cant admit this so they use euphemisms like, without background. Weirdly, it sounds like she's saying that the qualities of being smart, ambitious, hard working, thoughtful, sometimes very accomplished aren't what gets you into the real Harvard. Rather, you need the proper background.
These Rufo critics ended up damaging the Harvard brand even more (and by extension, the brand of elite universities in general). This occurred for two reasons. First, people generally detest snobs. Second, their criticisms of Rufo exposed how hollow the supposed egalitarian dogma of elite universities truly is. You cant say youre for equality and then say anyone who doesnt have the same degree as you is unworthy. You cant have it both ways. You either take responsibility for being a member of the elite and all of the responsibility that entails, or you can say youre the same as anybody else and thereby relinquish your duties. Prestige and influence require distinctions. In a truly equitable world, the Ivy League does not exist. These people have developed a kind of elite double-consciousness. In fact, its a mark of elite status to play this gamedecrying privilege while exploiting it. To claim to dismantle institutions while striving for power within them.
Our elites want it both ways. They want all of the privilege of being a member of this rarefied segment of society with none of the accompanying responsibilities. Why would they have any special responsibilities? Theyre the same as anybody else, after all.
Sometimes they do decide to anoint themselves with special duties. But instead of promoting values that would actually improve the lives of those they purport to care about, they instead focus on advancing dogmas that earn them plaudits or advance their own professional interests.
