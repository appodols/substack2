[In square brackets are thoughts/notes I jotted while listening]:
At the outset, Jordan says this sixth lecture will depart from a strict discussion of a specific Big Five trait and go into some of the societal ramifications of personality differences in general, and differences between men and women in particular.
This lecture comes after the previous two lectures on Neuroticism and Agreeableness. These are the two personality traits for which the sex differences between men and women are the largest. This is true across cultures.
The Big Five traits were established not through an a priori theory. Rather, this model of personality was discovered through brute force statistical methods. Early personality researchers collected a bunch of descriptors people used in everyday conversation to describe others. Through various statistical procedures, they found that these descriptive terms clustered into 5 categories. Since then, other researchers have used different methods and found the same 5 basic personality traits.
Jordan says that it took him several years to warm up to this understanding of personality. He thought it was an ugly model arrived at via inelegant means, but gradually believed it was the best way to understand human personality.
This is in part because these 5 personality traits were accurate predictors of real-world outcomes, and because researchers established connections between the personality traits and their underlying neurobiology.
The biggest sex difference in personality is in agreeableness, followed by neuroticism.
Still, its important to understand that men and women are more alike than they are different, especially at the trait level.
[Small differences add up, though. A useful analogy: When you look at isolated facial features, its hard to tell the difference between, say, a male nose and a female nose. But viewed as a whole, you can almost always tell the difference between a male face and a female face. Adult female and male faces are distinguished by dozens of tiny individual morphological differences. These add up. The same is true for personality. At the trait level, differences are relatively small; but collectively, men and women behave very differently in terms of personality.]
Jordan suggests that if we are looking for diversity, we would be better off recruiting people based on differing personality traits and temperament rather than focusing on gender or ethnicity.
Countries with greater sociopolitical equality and gender egalitarianism show the largest sex differences in personality traits between men and women.
[For instance, men and women in countries like Denmark and Sweden are more different from one another than men and women in more traditional societies like Vietnam and Botswana].
[This association between sociopolitical equality and larger sex differences is true for the Big Five. It is also true for the Dark Triad personality traits. It is also true for frequency of cryingwomen across cultures cry more than men, and this sex difference is especially pronounced in countries with high sociopolitical equality.]
[My friendSteve Stewart-Williamshas summed up this line of research: When you treat men and women differently, it makes them more similar. When you treat men and women similarly, it makes them more different.]

[You can see this with preferences as well. In developing countries, women are far more likely to study engineering and other lucrative disciplines. In rich countries, where economic security is more guaranteed, women are apt to choose less remunerative majors like communications or psychology (at Cambridge, I was sometimes literally the only male in the lecture hall).]
[One reason researchers have suggested for why sex differences correlate with sociopolitical equality is that in relatively rich and free societies, people are more able to fully express their underlying traits and preferences. But in less affluent and less egalitarian societies, people are expected to behave in a more rigid manner, which compresses sex differences.]
[Western societies believed that by treating men and women the same, naturally their interests and preferences would converge. Equality is good. Gender diversity is good. Smart people thought these two things would naturally go together. But the world doesnt work that way. Ive writtenhereabout this fundamental error.]

[However, in more egalitarian societies, men tend to change more than women. In other words, differences are relatively small between women in various societies. The magnitude of the differences between men is larger. I have speculated that perhaps its not that egalitarian societies make men more different. Rather, perhaps psychologically peculiar men are more likely to establish societies that are, or have the potential to become, relatively egalitarian. This is pure speculation on my part.]
[Even sex differences in height, BMI, obesity, and blood pressure are larger in countries with more egalitarian sex role socialization. In materially scarce environments, mens physicality is more suppressed, relative to women. To simplify, men have the physical capacity to grow and change more so than women. Material abundancenamely, adequate food and nutritionallows these underlying physical traits to be expressed.]

Jordan emphasizes the point that we are, of course, biological creatures. We share similar physiology and emotional systems to other mammals. Its not surprising that differences between males and females for the human organism exist.
Women are underrepresented in the STEM fields overall, in particular in the higher echelons. But in universities, women are now overrepresented as STEM students. This is a recent phenomenon that has occurred only in the last few years. [This is likely because fewer men are attending college nowadays.]
Individuals have different levels of occupational interest in people versus things. [A social worker is generally more interested in working with people; a physicist is generally more interested in working with things.]
Agreeable people are generally more interested in working with people. Disagreeable people are generally more interested in working with things.
Men and women show a large difference in interest in people vs. things. Men are more interested in things, women are more interested in people.
The increased male variability hypothesis: The average IQ for men and women is the same. But the male distribution has fatter tails, meaning that there are more men who have very low IQs and more men who have very high IQs. [Steven Pinker has summarized this as more prodigies, more idiots among males.]
[There is generally a larger proportion of males with learning disabilities and intellectual impairments compared with women. There is also a larger proportion of males with extremely high intelligence compared with women.]
[Theoverallmale to female ratio of intellectual disability is about 1.5:1. In the top 2% of the distribution (above 130 IQ), the ratio of males to females is about 1.4:1. In the top 0.1% (IQ above 140), the ratio is slightly more than 2:1.]

male to female ratio of intellectual disability is about 1.5:1. In the top 2% of the distribution (above 130 IQ), the ratio of males to females is about 1.4:1. In the top 0.1% (IQ above 140), the ratio is slightly more than 2:1.]
[This greater variability among men has been found for other traits as well. I recall reading that this is true for cleanliness and hygienethere are more men who will go days without cleaning themselves or showering, but there are also more men with a severe obsessive preoccupation with cleanliness and order. Same for conscientiousnessmen and women, on average, score the same on this trait. But there are more extremely lazy males and more male workaholics.]
Greater male variability has been found among animals too. Evolution is more likely to tinker with male traits because males are more disposable.
[Basically, males are kind of a wildcard.]
[Small average differences are associated with very large differences at the tail ends of a distribution. Men are more physically aggressive, on average, than women. But there is a lot of overlap in aggression between the sexes. However, at the extreme right tail of hyper-aggression, there are far more men than women. The most physically aggressive people in society are always male. This is why, across societies, males encompass 90-95% of prison inmates.]
[This is also true for height. Men are, on average, 5 inches taller than women. A noticeable difference, but not huge. But if you look at the extreme right tail of people who are 7 feet or taller, the ratio of males to females is something like 1000:1.]
[At the extreme ends, there are more risk-taking males than females. You see these men in the tails of the distribution and assume all males are like that. Not the case. Men are, on average, a bit more risk taking and adventurous than women. But not by much. The average man isnt that different from the average woman in terms of conventionally masculine psychological traits (physical courage, assertiveness, risk taking etc.). Different societies developed ways to inculcate such traits in men.]
Small average differences likely give rise to differences in the people vs. things occupations. Men who are smart and very preoccupied with things outnumber equally smart women (who tend to be interested in people) which helps to explain why there are more male engineers and female nurses.
Jordan stresses how important it is to have a bit of statistical sophistication and understand this point. Small average differences imply large differences at the tails. It is true that men and women are mostly the same, but at the extreme ends, there are very large sex differences. Were going to have to grow up and understand this.
Jordan suggests that there wont be a man left in the humanities and social sciences in 15 years. [This strikes me as unlikely. However, I have mentioned before that at Cambridge it was not uncommon for me to the only male in the lecture hall. More often, there were a few other males, but it was seldom more than, say, 20%. At the elite doctoral level in psychology, things are more balanced. A large proportion of male PhD students are not heterosexual though, suggesting even here there is a drop in conventional male representation. This is fine, of course, if it is a consequence of peoples preferences. But unlike in, say, engineering, where they make an active attempt to recruit women, and female students are encouraged to speak up, in psychology, this is not the case for men. ]
If its the case that leaving people to make free choices leads to inequality of outcome, then only totalitarian coercive measures (restricting freedom) can produce equal levels of representation across all social groups.
Jordan lists the most male-heavy jobs: Brick masons; concrete workers; electric powerline installers and repairers; heating, air conditioning, and refrigeration mechanics and installers; carpet finishers; iron and rebar workers; bus and truck mechanics; diesel engine specialists; roofers.
The most female-dominated occupation is exactly the same as it was in the 1950s: Secretaries and administrative assistants.
Other female-heavy jobs: childcare workers; receptionists and information clerks; teacher assistants; nurses; bookkeeping and accounting clerks; maids and housekeeping; personal and homecare aids; general office clerks.
It is interesting to note that there are no calls for equal gender representation in the occupations that are the most male dominated.
